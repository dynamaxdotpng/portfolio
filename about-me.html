Since quite an early age I've been fascinated with how technology enables society to
communicate and innovate. Even in the last 10 years, since I've really had a sense of the
world beyond home and school, technology has advanced hugely and it seems this is likely to
continue. When I was only 13 years old, I took part in the "Young Persons Festival of Code", a
coding competition held at Plymouth University. My team of 5 produced an app called "After Dark"
which helped children stay safe. We came second in the competition.

I'm self sufficient and passionate about a wide range of things. I raised around Â£2,500 for a
charity trip to Ghana when I was 15. This was part of a school exchange organised relationship
through the charity Partner Ghana. I visited two schools in Ghana over the course of two
weeks, living with them and helping them with school infrastructure projects. This was a
hugely humbling and rewarding experience, helping me understand the opportunity and privilege
I have. Back in the UK, I have had various part time hospitality jobs, including a managerial
role in Papa John's Pizza.

Over the course of the last year, I've had the privilege to volunteer with Solent NHS
regularly to help them design web services regarding sexual health information for teens. This
has really made me aware of the importance of the internet when it comes to educating people,
especially young people, such as myself.

Having a logical mind, I've always found maths straightforward. I've tutored Maths students
for the last 3 years, helping 5 GCSE students pass their exams and improve their grades. Most
of my students were predicted to get 3's and sometimes 2's, however, after practising and
putting in a lot of hard work, they've managed to reach 5's and 6's. This is something that
makes me incredibly proud, not just from what I managed to assist them with, but the pure
perseverance that young people can display when they put their minds to it.

As part of my T-Level course, I'm currently undertaking work experience with an SME web design
company. This is improving my understanding of several operating systems and coding languages
that I've never approached before.

I've completed a course in "Practical Podcasting" at college. This pushed me far out of my
comfort zone, working with others outside my normal friendship groups to be creative and
adapting to a branch of software I had never interacted with before.

From a young age, I've loved tech and pride myself on questioning how exactly anything and
everything works. I'm proud to have built and enhanced my own desktop computer at home over
the last two years. I regularly live stream, and I'm always playing with code.

I also really enjoy art, anime and all forms of digital creativity. I've combined this hobby
with streaming on twitch and video editing, and regularly attend Comic Con events with
friends. Using technology to engage and entertain people is something that I consider
important because I want everyone around me to be happy and everyone to feel included.

I'm interested in true crime and I'm also passionate about LGBTQ+ and race equality. Whilst
technology in general, and social media in particular, can be a force for good, it can also do
lots of harm to the vulnerable and create mental health issues. There is a dark side to
technology that worries me and it's something I want to do something about. I find cyber
security interesting because of the endless positive benefits it can inflict on people's lives
without them even noticing.

Technology advances can leave the more vulnerable and marginalised behind, and even worse, can
provide the opportunity for criminals to exploit others, or for hate speech and oppression. I
feel as if it's the role of my generation to find ways to stop this where we can. My ultimate
goal is to work in cyber security and computer science to help others in a crime prevention or
crime detection career, the security services, or possibly even health care.
